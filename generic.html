Phishing Patterns (Visual Guide)

Phishing attacks rely on tricking people into clicking, trusting, or revealing information. Understanding the visual patterns helps you identify threats faster.

1. Fake Login Pages

Scammers recreate real login screens to steal your credentials. Always verify the URL — small changes or extra characters are common red flags.

2. Urgent Action Scams

Attackers use fear: “Your account will be closed!” or “Suspicious activity detected!” These messages pressure you into acting without thinking.

3. Suspicious Attachments

Attachments disguised as invoices or receipts may contain malware. If you weren’t expecting a file — don’t open it.

4. Prize / Reward Scams

“You won!” scams try to harvest your personal info. Real companies don’t ask for private details to claim a prize.

5. Impersonation of Trusted Brands

Phishers use copied logos and design to appear legitimate. Always inspect the sender’s email and the domain they link to.
