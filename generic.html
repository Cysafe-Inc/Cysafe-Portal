Understanding Phishing Patterns

Phishing remains the most common and effective cyberattack used by scammers.

Below are the most common phishing patterns along with images and explanations.

1. Fake Login Pages

Fake login pages mimic legitimate websites to steal your credentials. Always check the URL before entering sensitive information.

2. Urgent Action Scams

Scammers create a sense of urgency to trick you into acting quickly without thinking. Always verify the source before responding.

3. Suspicious Attachments

Attachments may contain malware or viruses. Do not open unexpected attachments, especially from unknown senders.

4. Prize or Reward Scams

Be cautious of messages claiming you've won a prize. Legitimate organizations will not ask for personal information to claim rewards.

5. Impersonation of Trusted Brands

Scammers often impersonate well-known brands to gain your trust. Always verify the sender's email address and look for signs of phishing.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Enable multi-factor authentication whenever possible.
  • Do not click on unexpected or shortened links.
  • Use secure password managers.
  • Verify emails from official websites.
  • Stay updated with new phishing tactics.
